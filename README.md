# Bank Customer Churn Analysis

## 📌Overview:

Customer churn is a major challenge for banks, as losing customers directly impacts revenue. This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior to identify key factors that lead to churn and provide actionable insights.

The goal is to understand why customers leave and help the bank improve retention strategies.

## 🎯Objectives:
- Identify patterns and trends in customer churn.

- Analyze customer demographics and behavior.

- Detect key factors influencing churn.

- Build insights for improving customer retention.

## 📂Data sources:
Banking / Financial Services

Hugging Face (SunnyShaurya – Bank Customer Churn Dataset)

## 🛠️Tools & Technologies:

- Python

- Pandas

- NumPy

- Matplotlib

- Seaborn

- Power BI 

## 🔗Data Cleaning & Preprocessing:

- Removed duplicate records.

- Handled missing/null values.

- Dropped irrelevant columns: CustomerId, Surname.

- Grouped customers by tenure and age.

## 📊Key Insights:
- Inactive customers have a significantly higher churn rate.

- Customers with fewer products are more likely to leave.

- Higher balance customers show higher churn in some segments.

- Age plays a crucial role — older customers churn more.

## 💡Recommendations:

The churn analysis reveals that customer retention is strongly linked to engagement and product usage. Inactive customers and those with limited interaction with the bank are more likely to churn.
